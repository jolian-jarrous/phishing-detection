Duplicate analysis
==================
Total rows in raw dataset:       10000

Full-row duplicates (identical across ALL columns, including Domain):
  duplicated rows:      5626
  unique full rows:     4374

Feature-vector duplicates (identical AFTER dropping Domain column):
  duplicated rows:      9120
  unique feature vectors: 880

Interpretation:

The full-row count (5,626 out of 10,000) already shows a serious data
quality problem: over half the rows in the raw CSV are literal duplicates
of another row, same Domain, same 17 features, same label. The dataset
does not even contain 10,000 distinct URLs.

The feature-vector count is even more extreme. Once we strip the Domain
column and look only at the 17 numerical features (which is all the model
ever sees), only 880 unique feature vectors remain out of 10,000 rows.
That means most of the URLs that ARE unique at the string level collapse
into a much smaller number of feature-vector patterns because the feature
representation is so coarse.

For cross-validation this matters because the model sees feature vectors,
not URL strings. Two "different" URLs mapping to the same feature vector
end up in both train and test folds during CV, which artificially inflates
every score.
